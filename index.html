A gallery containing beautiful images of the city of San Antonio, TX. Including just a few of the many places to visit in the city.

PEARL

A historical district and culture hub in the center of San Antonio, Pearl offers a variety of restaurants, shops, and businesses. During the weekends, people can enjoy the outdoor Farmer's Market (Saturday) and Maker's Market (Sunday), all while enjoying the family and pet friendly environment.

JAPANESE TEA GARDEN

Scenic park from 1901 with stone bridges, a waterfall, koi ponds, a pagoda & the Jingu House Cafe. Shaped into a complex that included walkways, stone arch bridges, an island and a Japanese pavilion, the garden was termed the lily pond, and local residents donated bulbs to beautify the area. Exotic plants were provided by the City nursery and the City Public Service Company donated the lighting system. The pavilion was roofed with palm leaves from trees in City parks.

RIVER WALK

Iconic area with landscaped walkways, bridges, restaurants, bars, hotels, shops & boat tours. The San Antonio River Walk is home to countless special events. Open 365 days a year, it’s a place to relax, celebrate, dine, and drink.

ALAMO DRAFTHOUSE

Founded in Austin, TX in 1997, Alamo Drafthouse is known for creating awesome movie experiences. With servers always available to take orders and regular screenings of classic movies on film, this theater creates a better movie-going experience than other chains. The delicious and extensive menu alone is worth a visit.

THE SHOPS AT LA CANTERA

The Shops at La Cantera is a shopping center located within the La Cantera District of San Antonio. Having a unique design, this San Antonio mall boasts an impressive assortment of mid to high-end retailers and restaurants.

TOWER OF THE AMERICAS

750-ft. landmark with an observation deck, revolving eatery, stationary bar & a 4D adventure ride. Located in downtown San Antonio, the Tower of the Americas provides guests the most spectacular view of the Alamo City.
